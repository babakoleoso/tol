START — The Four Entry Pillars

Orientation • Definitions • Boundaries

SGI Rule: These are not competing religions. They are aligned functions of one cosmology. SwamiG Institute ensures the student receives power, law, and knowledge with containment.

1 Orisha — power in form
2 Kemet — law behind power

Pillar Function What it protects you from
Orisha Embodied divine forces; nature intelligence; lineage spirituality; ritual expression. Disconnection from life, body, community; power without grounding.

Correspondence — Not Competition

Comparative clarity for curriculum

SGI Policy: Correspondence is used for education and alignment. It is not used to rank traditions, replace cultures, or inflate identity.

  • Purpose: shared principles, different languages
  • Method: compare function → confirm ethics → validate results
  • Rule: power must return to service, not personal status

The Three Realms

Human • Natural • Cosmic

All work occurs across three realms. Movement between realms must be authorized, disciplined, and ethically contained.

  • Human Realm: body, daily life, ethics, service, relationships
  • Natural Realm: forces, elements, cycles, Orisha operations
  • Cosmic Realm: law, destiny, divine intelligence, witness truth

SGI Standard: We do not “escape” the human realm. We refine it. Any ascent that does not return as service is incomplete.

STOP — Seal, Close, Return

The mastery principle

STOP is the most important doctrine in Module 0. It means: seal knowledge, end ego ascent, and return wisdom to ethical service.

  • STOP is not fear. It is discipline.
  • STOP is not limitation. It is governance.
  • STOP is not denial. It is completion.

Closing Statement:
“Before you seek knowledge, learn limits. Before you rise, learn where to stop.”
